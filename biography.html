Biography

⌕

Ricardo Wagner is a Dominican multidisciplinary visual artist based in Santo Domingo. His artistic practice moves fluidly between oil and acrylic painting, mixed media, drawing, watercolor and photography.

His visual language is rooted in abstraction and informalism, exploring the origins of existence, the evolution of humanity and the spiritual tension between the infinite and the tangible world.

Originally trained in advertising and communication for development, Wagner’s background in graphic arts and printing deeply shaped his sensitivity to structure, material and visual rhythm. He is founder of HD Graph Estudio Gráfico and Bremen Printing.

Throughout his career he has received awards in art competitions and his work has been featured in Dominican media including Hoy, El Caribe, Diario Libre, Listín Diario and Noticias SIN.

His work seeks not to describe reality but to evoke it — creating spaces where the viewer encounters silence, mystery and contemplation through gesture, color and texture.

⌕

Statement

My painting emerges from the search for origin and presence. I work from intuition, allowing gesture and material to guide the evolution of the image.

Abstraction allows me to approach what cannot be represented directly — the invisible forces that shape human existence and our connection with nature and the cosmos.

Each work is a space of tension between control and surrender, where the sacred and the earthly coexist. I do not aim to impose meaning, but to open a contemplative field where viewers can encounter their own reflections.

Painting, for me, is an act of listening — a dialogue between matter, time and spirit.
